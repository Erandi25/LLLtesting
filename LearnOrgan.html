What is Organ Donation?

Organ donation is the process of donating your organs to save or enhance the lives of others. It is a selfless act that can transform the lives of those in need of transplants.





Why Should You Donate Organs?

  • Every organ donor can save up to 8 lives.
  • Millions of people around the world are waiting for transplants.
  • Your donation gives a second chance to those in need.

Vital Organs for Donation:

  • Heart: Helps save patients with heart failure or severe heart diseases.
  • Lungs: Treats individuals with severe lung diseases like COPD or pulmonary fibrosis.
  • Liver: Can be transplanted entirely or split into sections to save multiple lives.
  • Kidneys: Helps those suffering from kidney failure or chronic kidney disease.
  • Pancreas: Helps treat or cure diabetes by replacing a failing pancreas.
  • Intestines: Transplanted in cases of severe intestinal failure.


Other Tissues and Cells That Can Be Donated:

  • Corneas: Restore sight to those with corneal damage or blindness.
  • Skin: Used for burn victims and reconstructive surgeries.
  • Bone and Bone Marrow: Help patients with bone diseases or surgeries.
  • Heart Valves: Save patients with heart valve defects.
  • Tendons and Ligaments: Aid in reconstructive surgeries.

How to Become an Organ Donor

Becoming an organ donor is simple:

  1. Register with your local organ donation organization.
  2. Inform your family of your decision.
  3. Carry an organ donor card at all times.
