REQUIREMENT FOR DONORS

To ensure the safety of both patients and donors, these are some of the requirements donors must meet to be eligible to donate blood based on their donation type.

Requirements for blood donation

  • Donors should be at least 17 years old.
  • Donors typically need to weight at least
    110 pounds (50 kg).
  • Donors must be in good health and free
    infections or ilness on the day of donation.
  • Normal blood pressure and sufficient hemoglobin
    levels are required.
  • Certain high-risk behaviors (such as
    drug use or unprotected sex) can disqualify a
    person from donating blood.
  • Whole blood donors can usually donate every
    56 days (8 weeks).

Requirements for organ donation

  • Individuals with certain medical conditions
    may not be eligible to donate organs.
  • Individuals who have received certain
    vaccinations may need to wait a specific period
    before donating.
  • Donors should be informed of the potential
    risks and complications associated with the
    donation process, especially for living donors.
  • There is no strict age limit for organ donation
  • Organ donation requires explicit consent.
    This can be done through a registry or by
    informing family members of one’s wishes.
