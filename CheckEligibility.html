Are You Eligible to Donate Blood or Organs?

Donating blood or organs can save lives, but it’s important to know if you're eligible. Check if you meet the requirements for donation.



Blood Donation Eligibility Criteria:




  • Age: You must be between 18-65 years old (some regions may allow 17-year-olds with parental consent).
  • Weight: You must weigh at least 50 kg (110 lbs).
  • Health: You should be in good health, with no cold, flu, or other infections.
  • Frequency: You can donate blood once every 8 weeks for whole blood donation, or more often for other types like plasma.
  • Pregnancy: Women who are pregnant cannot donate blood.

Organ Donation Eligibility Criteria:




  • Age: Organ donation can happen at any age, but the health of the organs is more important than age.
  • Health: You must be free of certain medical conditions like HIV, active cancer, and other life-threatening illnesses.
  • Family Consent: In the case of deceased donation, family consent is necessary for donation to proceed.
  • Organ Function: Healthy organs are needed for donation, and each potential donor is evaluated for suitability.

How to Check Your Eligibility

Checking if you are eligible is simple:

  1. Contact your local blood donation center or organ transplant organization for specific criteria.
  2. Complete a health questionnaire at the time of donation.
  3. Consult with a medical professional if you have any doubts about your eligibility.
  4. Keep a record of your health and past donation history for reference.
